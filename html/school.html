All Things School:

My School Experience.

I started out as a Northern Virginia Community College student trying to find out what they wanted to do. On my way to earning a chemistry or biology degree I decided to make a switch to computer science. Now I am a soon-to-be graduate from James Madison University with a BS degree in Computer Science and a minor in Human Resource Development ready to meet new people and learn new things.

While at school I participated in multiple extra-curricular activities. At NOVA I was fervent about students having a place to go after a hard work week, so I participated in and eventually became joint president of "Game Knights". This club at NOVA would meet once a week to socalize around the games. We would participate in card games, board games, table-top games, video games, and more that could be organized. I learned about management and organizational responsibility from my time with them.

While at James Madison University I could not participate to the same level of involvement, however I attended meeting for both the Unix Users Group [UUG] and the Science Fiction and Fantasy Guild [SFFG]. In the Unix Users group I was able to learn more about Unix based systems, shell, editors, and C (programming language). The Science Fiction and Fantasy Guild was alot like the club I headed at NOVA giving students a place to come decompress after their hard work.
